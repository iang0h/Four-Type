# Know Your True Nature: The FourType Field Guide

**Status:** Approved on 2026-07-14  
**Formats:** Designed PDF and responsive EPUB  
**Target length:** Approximately 150 designed PDF pages  
**Primary channel:** Direct sale through fourtype.com

## Product Intent

The FourType Field Guide is the paid, practical next step after the free FourType temperament test. It is not another personality encyclopedia. It helps readers recognize how their temperament affects ordinary decisions, work, communication, relationships, conflict, stress, and personal growth.

The book should leave readers with two outcomes:

1. A more compassionate and precise understanding of their own patterns.
2. A usable way to understand and work with people whose patterns differ from theirs.

The book is universal and secular. It may discuss the history of temperament models, but it will not adopt a religious framework or present historical beliefs as modern science.

## Reader Journey

Readers are expected to take the FourType test before starting the book. The opening pages link and point to the free test at `fourtype.com/quiz`. The book then teaches readers to interpret their complete result, including primary and secondary temperament influences, without treating the result as a fixed identity.

The intended journey is:

1. Take the test.
2. Understand what temperament can and cannot explain.
3. Learn the four core patterns.
4. Study the reader's specific result or blend.
5. Learn how other people may experience the reader.
6. Apply the model to work, close relationships, conflict, and stress.
7. Choose a small, temperament-aware growth practice.

## Editorial Promise

The reader should encounter a useful observation, example, question, or tool on every spread. The prose should be warm, direct, specific, and easy to read. It should sound like an observant human guide rather than a clinical report, motivational speech, or automated personality description.

The book will:

- Explain patterns through familiar situations instead of jargon.
- Use balanced language that shows both the gift and cost of each tendency.
- Distinguish preference from ability and temperament from destiny.
- Include practical scripts, exercises, and examples.
- Avoid ranking types or presenting one type as healthier or more capable.
- Avoid diagnosing mental health, suitability for employment, or relationship success.

## Core-Type Chapter Template

Each six-page core-type chapter teaches the underlying pattern rather than duplicating a result profile.

1. Character-led opening and the type's central need.
2. How the type pays attention and makes sense of a situation.
3. Natural strengths and the conditions in which they appear.
4. Blind spots and how the same strengths can become costly.
5. Communication, work, and relationship needs.
6. Stress signals, recovery, and a first growth practice.

## Four-Page Profile Template

All 16 results receive equal editorial care. Each profile follows a stable four-page structure while using type-specific examples and visual storytelling.

### Page 1: The Inner Pattern

- A recognizable opening scene
- Primary drive and secondary influence
- What the reader notices first
- What feels natural but may be invisible to them
- A short "You may recognize this" reflection

### Page 2: Work and Communication

- Contribution at work
- Preferred pace and decision style
- Feedback and meeting behavior
- Conditions that help the reader do good work
- A script for communicating a need clearly

### Page 3: Love, Friendship, and Conflict

- How care is usually expressed
- What the reader tends to need from close relationships
- Common misunderstanding with another person
- Conflict behavior and repair
- A conversation prompt

### Page 4: Stress and Growth

- Early and late stress signals
- Defensive pattern
- What helps and what makes matters worse
- One blind spot worth watching
- A realistic seven-day growth practice

## Practical-Life Chapters

Part IV should teach skills across types rather than repeat profile descriptions. Planned topics include:

- Reading differences without mind-reading
- Asking for what you need
- Giving feedback that another type can hear
- Receiving criticism without immediately defending or withdrawing
- Decision-making in mixed teams
- Leadership without imposing one temperament's pace on everyone
- Collaboration, meetings, and division of responsibilities
- Friendship maintenance and social energy
- Intimacy, affection, reassurance, and independence
- Boundaries and resentment
- Conflict escalation and de-escalation
- Apology, repair, and rebuilding trust

Each chapter should include a realistic scene involving two or more FourType characters, a diagram or comparison tool, and one action the reader can try.

## Research and Originality Standard

The uploaded books are research inputs, not structural or prose templates. Their useful themes may inform the research map, but no chapter will be rewritten from a source chapter and no distinctive wording, anecdotes, diagrams, quizzes, or branded frameworks will be copied.

Research will use a source ledger containing:

- Claim or teaching point
- Source and location
- Evidence strength
- Whether it is historical, observational, or supported by current research
- How the idea was transformed into original FourType instruction

Current psychological claims should prefer primary research, established reference works, or professional organizations. Popular books may supply questions and themes but should not be the sole support for scientific claims.

The manuscript will explicitly avoid or qualify:

- Direct one-to-one mappings between a temperament and a neurotransmitter or hormone
- Claims that all civilizations independently discovered the same four types
- Numerical accuracy claims not supported by validation data
- Claims that temperament predicts diagnosis, job performance, morality, or compatibility
- Speculative typing of public figures presented as fact

The final book will include a selected bibliography and direct readers to a fuller online research note when space prevents complete print notes.

## Voice and Language

The voice is humane, observant, and practical. It should be confident enough to guide without pretending the framework explains everything.

Preferred qualities:

- Short paragraphs and concrete examples
- Ordinary words over academic terminology
- Gentle humor used sparingly
- Specific behavior rather than flattering abstractions
- Second-person writing that invites reflection rather than declares identity
- Clear distinction between "may," "often," and "always"

Avoid:

- Inflated promises and mystical certainty
- Formulaic lists of three on every page
- Repetitive "superpower" and "kryptonite" language
- Gender stereotypes
- Moral judgments hidden inside type descriptions
- Artificially dramatic or therapy-like language

## Visual Direction

The PDF uses a 7 x 10 inch portrait format that works as a premium digital book and can later be adapted for print. The visual identity combines the FourType quest world with the clarity of a modern field guide.

### Brand System

- Foundation: obsidian, warm parchment, and restrained gold
- Commander: red with supporting charcoal and warm metal
- Bard: amber with supporting coral and cream
- Strategist: blue with supporting ink and cool silver
- Guardian: green with supporting moss and warm stone
- Display typography: a readable classical serif with restrained fantasy character
- Body typography: a highly legible book serif or humanist sans serif

The palette must remain balanced; gold and dark backgrounds are accents, not the only page treatment. Long reading sections use light backgrounds and strong contrast.

### Visual Rhythm

Every page includes a meaningful visual anchor, which may be:

- A character illustration or interaction scene
- A simple behavioral diagram
- A comparison table
- An icon-led checklist
- A script or conversation card
- A reflection worksheet
- A timeline, spectrum, or decision map
- A restrained decorative navigation element

Full-page or full-spread illustrations are reserved for the cover, part openings, core-type openings, and important real-life scenes. Utility pages use smaller character poses, icons, and diagrams so the book remains readable and the artwork continues to feel special.

Existing FourType character assets establish appearance, clothing, colors, and personality. New scenes must preserve those identities rather than redesigning the cast.

## EPUB Strategy

The EPUB contains the same complete editorial content but is reflowable rather than a fixed replica of the PDF. It will include:

- Semantic HTML chapters and headings
- Working navigation and table of contents
- Responsive optimized images
- Alt text for meaningful illustrations and diagrams
- EPUB-safe type and color fallbacks
- Page-break guidance for chapter openings
- Accessible reading order
- Internal links and external links to FourType resources

Complex PDF diagrams will receive simplified EPUB versions when a fixed layout would become unreadable on a phone.

## Website Integration

The opening pages link to `fourtype.com/quiz`. Relevant chapters may link to result pages, compatibility tools, and further learning on FourType. Links should be useful and sparse rather than turning the book into a catalogue.

The direct-sale flow is outside the manuscript's first production milestone, but the files should support later delivery through the FourType website. The PDF will include metadata and a branded cover suitable for checkout and library previews.
